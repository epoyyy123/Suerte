“I’ve been using iPhones for years, but the iPhone 13 left me underwhelmed. While the performance is as smooth as always, the design hasn’t changed much, and it feels like Apple is just rehashing old ideas. The camera is good, but it’s nothing revolutionary – other phones on the market offer similar or better results at a lower price. Also, I’m really tired of the Lightning port. Apple is known for charging a premium, but in 2022, it feels like they’re making users pay for outdated tech. The lack of significant innovation and the high price tag make it hard to recommend unless you’re already heavily invested in the Apple ecosystem.”

“The iPhone 14 is a solid phone with great performance, but it’s starting to feel a bit outdated compared to the competition. The camera quality is fantastic, especially for social media and general photography, but when you compare it to the latest Android flagships, it lacks some of the innovation seen in the newer models. Battery life is decent, but not exceptional, and it still relies on Lightning instead of USB-C, which is a bit frustrating when you own other devices with USB-C ports. Overall, it's a reliable iPhone, but if you're looking for cutting-edge features, you might want to consider the newer models or even a different brand.”

Grace Lei
